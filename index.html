- Education -

Lucia Eve Berger holds a specialized graduate degree (Diplômes d'études supérieures spécialisées, DESS) in Machine Learning from MILA/Université de Montreal (2023), a masters in Software Engineering & Computer Science jointly awarded by TU Berlin, TU Kaiserslautern and University of Bolzano (2019), and a bachelor in Computer Science and Economics from McGill University (2015).

For her first masters, she was ranked first on acceptance and is a recipient of the Erasmus Mundus Scholarship ($48,000). She completed her thesis on Traffic Sign Recognition with Fraunhofer Institute for Experimental Software Engineering (2019). In 2023, she finished a second graduate degree from MILA (Quebec Artificial Intelligence Institute)/Université de Montréal while working full-time. She was awarded RBC Future launch scholarship ($3000). Her research focused on Automatic Speech Recognition and she completed an 8-month project with Microsoft on Minimum Word Error Rate Training.

- Professional -

From 2015 to 2017, she participated in the Edison Engineering Rotational Programme at General Electric (2 years). She worked on sustainable lighting networks and wind turbine implementation. From 2017 to 2019, she researched at the German Institute for Artificial Intelligence (DFKI) with Siemens Energy . Her work was focused on centralizing turbine data and smart grid pattens using machine learning. Since August 2019, she has been employed as a Research Scientist at Microsoft, in the Applied Artificial Intelligence division. She worked on Automatic Speech Recognition and now builds software assistants using AI.

- Interests -

She has lived in the USA (Lexington), Canada (Montreal), Mexico (Guadalajara), Italy (Bolzano) and Germany (Berlin). She speaks English, French, Spanish and German at a professional level. She studies Italian and Japanese for fun! She enjoys the outdoors, including hiking, skiing and backpacking.
